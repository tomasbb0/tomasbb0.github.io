THE CATEGORY WEDGE

Observation shows the sequence.
Verification reveals the operating logic.

Screen and system traces are valuable evidence. They do not, by themselves, prove who has authority, which policy governs a decision, whether a workaround is legitimate, or what should happen in a rare exception. Company Brain triangulates employee explanation, approved documents, and selected system evidence.

01

Recursive coverage

Start with leadership, follow verified reporting lines, then resolve the cross-functional relationships that make real work happen.

02

Accountable semantics

Attach owners, approval thresholds, policies, permissions, exceptions, escalation routes, outputs, and definitions of done.

03

Contradiction resolution

Detect conflicting accounts, missing ownership, and unexplained handoffs—then ask the person best positioned to resolve them.

04

Delivery-ready handoff

Package the current process, decision rules, integrations, access needs, evaluation cases, success metrics, and unresolved risks.

05

Multimodal evidence

Verify explanations against approved documents, system events, and privacy-preserving desktop observations—with redaction and employee controls.

06

Deployed-AI control

Maintain agent owners, permissions, approval gates, evaluations, incidents, reversibility, and measured outcomes in the same graph.

WHERE WE START

High-value work that is hardest to infer from a screen.

Observation-first products scale fastest when work is frequent, digital, repeatable, and performed inside approved applications. Our entry wedge is the inverse—not because those tools are weak, but because the missing context is most economically important here.
